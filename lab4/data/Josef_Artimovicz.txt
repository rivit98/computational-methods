Josef Artimovicz (born 7 January 1909; died 11 April 1988) was an Austrian footballer who played in the second half of the 1930's and in the 1940's. He played as Striker. 
Born in Vienna, Artimovicz played for ASV Hertha Wien, First Vienna FC and Favoritner SC in the Austrian capital, before he joined Young Boys in Switzerland. He played one season for the Young Boys and in his 16 games he scored ten goals.
Artimovicz joined FC Basel in 1935. After two test games, against local club FC Birsfelden and the french team FC Mulhouse (in both games he scored a goal) he played his domestic league debut for Basel in the away game against St. Gallen on 25 August 1935. Although he scored his first league goal for his new club in this game, it did not save the team from being defeted by two goals to three. In his one and a half years playng for Basel, Artimovicz played a total of 39 games, scoring a total of 22 goals. 26 of these games were in the Nationalliga, two in the Swiss Cup and 11 were friendly games. He scored 14 goal in the domestic league and the other eight were scored during the test games. Artimovicz was the clubs top league goal scorer in the 1935/36 Nationalliga season.

Methuselah was an English rock, founded and active in the 1960s.


== Overview ==
The line-up included Mick Bradley (drums), who came from The Sorrows and left to join Steamhammer in 1969. John Gladwin and Terry Wincott were school friends from Scunthorpe.The origins of the band were in two earlier 1960s bands, The Dimples and Gospel Garden. The group renamed itself to Methuselah. Influences included folk music and gospel music. The band were signed to Elektra Records in the late 1960s, with a three-LP contract.
John Gladwin and Terry Wincott were later in the folkband Amazing Blondel.


== Members ==
The quintet consisted of:
Craig Austin – bass, vocals
Mick Bradley – drums (died 1972)
John Gladwin – vocals, vibes
Les Nicol – guitar, vocals
Terry Wincott – guitar, vocals

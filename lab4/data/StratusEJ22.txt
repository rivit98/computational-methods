The Stratus EJ 22 is an American aircraft engine, produced by Stratus 2000 of Corvallis, Oregon for use in homebuilt aircraft.


== Design and development ==
The engine is based upon the Subaru EJ 22 automotive engine. It is a four-cylinder four-stroke, 2,200 cc (134.3 cu in), liquid-cooled, gasoline engine design, with a belt-type reduction drive with a reduction ratio of 2.2:1. It employs electronic ignition and produces 180 hp (134 kW) at 5900 rpm.The company seems to have gone out of business about 2008 and production ended.

Data from World Directory of Leisure Aviation 2011-12


=== General characteristics ===
Type: Four-cylinder, four stroke aircraft engine
Bore: 96.9 mm (3.8 in)
Stroke: 75 mm (3.0 in)
Displacement: 2,200 cc (134.3 cu in)
Dry weight: 120 kg (264.6 lb)


=== Components ===
Fuel type: gasoline
Cooling system: liquid
Reduction gear: belt-type reduction drive, reduction ratio 2.2:1


=== Performance ===
Power output: 180 hp (134 kW) at 5900 rpm
Compression ratio: 9:1

Martin Neumann (born 27 January 1956) is a German politician. Born in Vetschau, Brandenburg, he represents the Free Democratic Party (FDP). Martin Neumann has served as a member of the Bundestag from the state of Brandenburg from 2009 until 2013 and since 2017.


== Life ==
After studying technical building equipment at the Technical University of Dresden, Neumann was awarded a doctorate in 1987 as Dr.-Ing. at the Cottbus University of Engineering after successfully defending his dissertation on the subject of investigating the thermal insulation behaviour of heavy ventilated exterior wall structures. From 1987 to 1990, Neumann worked as the urban planning director in LÃ¼bbenau. Since 1995, Neumann has been a sworn expert for building services, heat, cold and noise insulation. From 1997 to 1999, Neumann was a lecturer for supply engineering and technical building equipment at the Lausitz University of Applied Sciences and the University of Applied Sciences Magdeburg-Stendal. In 1999, he accepted a call to the University of Applied Sciences Magdeburg-Stendal as professor for technical building services. He is also an energy consultant for the consumer advice centre.  He became member of the bundestag after the 2017 German federal election. He is a member of the Committee for Economy and Energy and spokesman for energy policy of the FDP parliamentary group in the Bundestag.
